TRADE BASICS

Trading involves buying and selling financial assets like stocks, bonds, and currencies to earn a profit. Below are some fundamental concepts to help you get started:

  • Stock Market: A platform where shares of companies are bought and sold.
  • Types of Trading:
    • Day Trading: Buying and selling within the same day.
    • Swing Trading: Holding positions for a few days to weeks.
    • Long-Term Investing: Holding assets for months or years.
  • Key Terms:
    • Broker: A platform or individual that facilitates trades.
    • Buy/Sell Orders: Instructions to purchase or sell an asset at a specific price.
    • Stop-Loss: A tool to minimize losses by setting a threshold.
    • Leverage: Borrowing funds to amplify potential returns (and risks).
    • Market Volatility: The degree of price fluctuations in the market.
    • Bid/Ask Spread: The difference between the buying and selling price.
  • Start Small: Use demo accounts to practice trading without risking real money.
  • Technical Analysis:
    • Analyzing price charts to identify patterns and trends.
    • Common tools: Moving Averages, RSI (Relative Strength Index), Bollinger Bands.
  • Fundamental Analysis: Evaluating the financial health and prospects of a company.
  • Risk Management:
    • Never risk more than 1-2% of your trading capital per trade.
    • Always set stop-loss levels to limit potential losses.
  • Emotional Discipline: Avoid trading based on fear or greed; stick to your strategy.
  • Keep Updated: Stay informed about global news and economic trends that affect markets.

Remember, Trading involves risks. Always do thorough research before investing.
